Indian Cuisine

A Symphony of Spices

Indian cuisine is a vibrant tapestry of flavors, colors, and aromas that varies dramatically from region to region. Known for its sophisticated use of spices and herbs, Indian food offers an incredible diversity of vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes.

From the creamy curries of the north to the coconut-based dishes of the south, from the seafood specialties of the coasts to the wheat-based breads of the plains, Indian cuisine is as diverse as its geography and cultures.

Many Indian spices like turmeric, cumin, and coriander are now widely available in Ghana, making it easier than ever to recreate authentic flavors at home. The cooking techniques are also adaptable to Ghanaian kitchens.

Signature Indian Dishes

Butter Chicken

Spice Level:

This creamy tomato-based curry from Punjab features tender chicken pieces in a rich sauce made with butter, cream, and aromatic spices.

Ghanaian Tip: Use local chicken and substitute fresh cream with evaporated milk for a lighter version.

Biryani

Spice Level:

A fragrant rice dish layered with marinated meat, caramelized onions, and aromatic spices, cooked together to create a flavorful one-pot meal.

Ghanaian Tip: Try making it with local jasmine rice and substitute traditional meats with goat or guinea fowl.

Dosa

Spice Level:

A thin, crispy fermented crepe made from rice and lentils, typically served with coconut chutney and sambar (lentil stew).

Ghanaian Tip: Ferment the batter in Ghana's warm climate for authentic flavor, and serve with local pepper sauces.

Regional Variations

North Indian

Features creamy curries, tandoori dishes, and breads like naan and roti. Uses dairy products like ghee and paneer.

South Indian

Known for rice-based dishes, dosas, idlis, and coconut-heavy curries. More vegetarian-focused with tangy flavors.

East Indian

Features fish, mustard oil, and sweets. Known for dishes like macher jhol (fish curry) and rasgulla.

West Indian

Includes Gujarati thalis, Goan seafood curries, and Maharashtrian street food like vada pav.

Ghanaian Adaptations

Spice Blends

Many Indian spice blends can be recreated with locally available spices. Garam masala can be made with Ghanaian cinnamon, cloves, and pepper. Curry powder blends well with local chili peppers.

Herb Substitutes

While fresh cilantro is ideal, local herbs like scent leaves can provide a similar freshness to dishes. Curry leaves can be substituted with bay leaves or omitted if unavailable.

Protein Options

Traditional Indian proteins like chicken, goat, and fish are readily available in Ghana. For vegetarian dishes, local beans and lentils work perfectly in dals and curries.

Featured Recipe: Ghanaian-Indian Fusion Chicken Curry

Fusion Chicken Curry with Local Flavors

45 mins Serves 4 Medium
Spice:

This recipe blends traditional Indian curry techniques with Ghanaian ingredients for a delicious fusion dish. The result is a flavorful, aromatic curry that's not too spicy but packed with flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 3 tomatoes, pureed
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tbsp curry powder (local or Indian)
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp suya spice (optional)
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro or scent leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle for 30 seconds.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Add garlic and ginger, sauté for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add chicken pieces and brown on all sides, about 5 minutes.
  5. Add all dry spices (curry powder, turmeric, coriander, suya spice) and stir well to coat the chicken.
  6. Add tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the mixture, about 5-7 minutes.
  7. Pour in coconut milk and bring to a simmer. Cover and cook for 20-25 minutes until chicken is tender.
  8. Adjust salt and spice levels to taste. If the curry is too thick, add a little water.
  9. Garnish with fresh herbs and serve with rice or local fufu.

Tip: For extra flavor, marinate the chicken with half the spices and some lemon juice for 30 minutes before cooking.
